# MISO Brand Direction

MISO is a premium modern Web3 ticketing platform for festivals, concerts, nightlife, and exclusive experiences where tickets are issued as NFTs.

## Identity

MISO should feel like Apple meets Boiler Room meets Ledger meets luxury nightlife: exclusive, premium, trustworthy, culturally relevant, technologically advanced, fashion-forward, and luxury but accessible.

The product speaks to Gen Z and young urban audiences who care about music, fashion, technology, ownership, and clean digital identity. It should communicate Web3 infrastructure without using crypto-cliche visuals.

## Palette

| Token | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Deep Black | `#121212` | Primary background, hero overlays, app shell |
| Carbon Black | `#111111` | Cards, dark UI surfaces, hover states |
| Champagne Beige | `#E6D8C9` | Primary text, secondary controls, editorial contrast |
| Soft White | `#F5F3EE` | High-emphasis text and clean highlights |
| Matte Gold | `#B89B5E` | Accent, focus states, collectible details |

Avoid cliché crypto neon, childish Web3 graphics, cartoon illustrations, meme-coin energy, gamer effects, and bright purple or blue startup gradients.

## Typography

Use modern luxury sans-serif typography. Preferred families are Neue Montreal, Satoshi, or Inter. In the current app, Inter is the implementation font.

Use minimal and bold hierarchy: large editorial headlines, medium-weight labels, restrained mono details for NFT/token metadata, and generous spacing.

## Logo Direction

The wordmark should be minimal, uppercase, and confident. Use the dot motif sparingly as a signal for access, verification, or live status. Avoid coin symbols, blockchain cubes, cartoon tickets, or overworked futuristic marks.

## UI Direction

Use a dark luxury interface with matte black surfaces, champagne text, subtle gold details, clean layouts, smooth mobile-first flows, rounded cards, and restrained micro interactions.

Screens should feel like a premium ticket wallet and event marketplace: event discovery, NFT collectible ticket cards, QR and NFT hybrid passes, secure resale, loyalty/rewards, VIP memberships, and official anti-scalping exchange.

## Website Direction

Lead with cinematic nightlife and culture. The first viewport should sell exclusive access and ownership, not protocol complexity. Use high-contrast event photography, bold event names, and clear CTAs for ticket purchase and marketplace discovery.

## Mobile App Direction

Prioritize event discovery, ticket wallet, collectible NFT pass details, resale status, QR verification, and rewards. Every pass should feel ownable and premium, with token metadata visible but not overwhelming.

## Social Direction

Use premium electronic music and fashion references: dark venues, cinematic lighting, backstage access, close-up wristbands, matte textures, gold details, artist/event typography, and high-contrast editorial crops.

Avoid hype-bro language, overexplaining crypto, glowing neon city clichés, meme visuals, and busy futuristic overlays.
